Personas & Storyboarding: CSCI 1300 Project 1

This is an assignment for CSCI 1300: User Interfaces and User Experience at Brown University. Over the course of the assignment, I investigated the usage and accessibility of the pool-table area in the student center at Brown. I was curious if the equipment Interfaces were obvious for different types of users (beginner, casual, repeat), and how affordances of the space affected the usage and demographics of the users. Below is documentation on my preparation, observations, and summaries.

Part 1: Preparation

Chosen Interface: Pool area (equipment and user ordering systems)

The pool area space has multiple interactive components. There are two equipment areas: the cue stick rack and within the pool tables themselves, which store the balls and the rack on a built-in shelf. Additionally, there is an implied social interface of tables nearby where waiting players can stand. The equipment interface solves the problem of storing equipment in between user turns and giving users access to different equipment options. The cue stick rack works by slotting the pole up through a hole and settling it down into a holster. The rack shelf is a hole in the side of the pool table, and the balls are stored in a central space that is fed to by the holes in the pool table, below the rack shelf.

  • How often do you use the pool table area?
  • What steps would you take to begin a game?
  • Is equipment usually where you expect it to be?
  • What steps would you take immediately when a game ends?
  • How often do you play with others?
  • Do you ever find the tables full when you're trying to use them? How do you approach that situation?

Part 2: Recording Observations

Observations

  • Some users brought their own additional equipment, such as notebooks or special gloves
  • Multiple users didn't place their cue sticks into the holster, opting to leave it to the side. Or, between a pair of users, one returned his stick and the other left it on the table.
  • One set of users started the game without using the rack
  • All users made sure that the remaining post-game balls were returned to the centralized ball space through the surface holes before leaving
  • Multiple pairs of users had one user set up the game while the other waited or talked to observers
  • A ratio of 8:1 male:female users used the space
  • Multiple users accidentally knocked overhead lamps askew with their poles while playing
  • Users who 'properly' put away their sticks ignored other sticks that were laying out near the cue stick rack
  1. How often do you use the pool table area?
    • One user responded that they use the area every other day, for about 4-5 games a session
    • Other users responded that they very infrequently use the pool table
  2. What steps would you take to begin a game?
    • All users responded with essentially the same sequence: that they would get cue sticks, set up the balls with the rack, put the rack away, and begin play.
    • These responses followed observed patterns - I did not see the rack left out during gameplay during any of my observation
  3. Is equipment usually where you expect it to be?
    • Experienced/frequent users noted that yes, equipment was where they needed it to be. None drew attention to the fact that cue sticks were left both on the table or the rack
    • Less experienced players noted that their first time playing in the pool area, it was difficult to locate the rack because its storage location is not visually obvious.
    • No experienced users noted bringing their own equipment, despite my observation that they did
  4. What steps would you take immediately when a game ends?
    • All users noted that they would put the cues sticks away
    • This is an interesting observation because in my experience, "putting the cue sticks away" took on multiple forms
    • No users noted that they would return all balls to the centralized area by knocking/pushing them into pockets, despite the fact that I witnessed every single user doing this after a game had concluded.
  5. How often do you play with others?
    • Casual users noted that they usually played with other people
    • More frequent users noted that they sometimes play alone, but also frequently play with other people
  6. Do you ever find the tables full when you're trying to use them? How do you approach that situation?
    • All users had noted that they had found tables full when trying to use them before
    • Infrequent users noted multiple responses to this situation: standing on the outskirts of the room until a table opened up, trying to look menacing or intentional to 'give a hint' to those playing that they wanted to use the space, or just leaving when they saw that the tables were occupied
    • More frequent users felt more comfortable approaching current players and asking "can I have next game"
